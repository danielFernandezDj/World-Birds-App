Meadowlark

Appearance: Medium-sized songbird with bright yellow breast, black V-shaped markings, and brown back with black streaks. Habitat: Found in open grasslands, meadows, and agricultural fields across North America. Song: Melodious and flute-like, consisting of clear, whistled notes. Behavior: Ground-dwelling, foraging for insects and seeds on the ground. Skilled at perching on elevated spots to sing.
